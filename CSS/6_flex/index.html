A forest is a complex ecosystem dominated by trees and other woody vegetation, typically defined by the United Nations as land spanning more than 0.5 hectares with trees higher than 5 meters and a canopy cover of more than 10%.Forests cover roughly one-third of the Earth's landmass and play a crucial role in regulating the global climate, maintaining the water cycle, and providing habitats for countless species.
